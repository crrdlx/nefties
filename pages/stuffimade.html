The Satoshi Bitcoin Converter app.

I made the Satoshi Bitcoin Converter in 2013 to help me navigate Bitcoin decimals and units. It's not exactly NFT-related, but it was the first thing I ever made with regards to Bitcoin or blockchain. And, of course, everything NFT is the daughter or granddaughter of Bitcoin.

Although it's a simple app, I'm kind of proud of it. It's been used in 130+ countries worldwide (map below). Many of those are developing nations. I believe that the abilities provided by Bitcoin and cryptocurrency help people in very real, tangible ways on a daily basis. My hope is that the Satoshi Bitcoin Converter helps as well in some small way.


Nations using the Satoshi Bitcoin Converter shown in glorious Bitcoin orange.

What started as a personal tool became the Satoshi Bitcoin Converter web app and an Android app.

Bitty Miner

From the throwback department, the first bitcoin/crypto thing I ever made was the Bitty Miner back in June 2012. It's a long story, but the upshot is this:

  • It was a small, simple html doohickey to mine bitcoin via browser.
  • It used bitcoinplus.com which looked like this back then.
  • I earned about .02 bitcoin.
  • I received about .01 bitcoin, then the bitcoinplus guy vanished.
  • The Bitty page is still live at http://bitty.wikidot.com/, including a video illustrating how it worked then. Rave music, say what?! (Bitty is still there, but no longer works because bitcoinplus.com went AWOL.)
  • The gory details and code are at this bitcointalk.org post.
